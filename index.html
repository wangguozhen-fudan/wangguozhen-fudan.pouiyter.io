I study algebraic topology. I am especially interested in understanding homotopy groups. Currently I am working on several aspects of homotopy theory:
  • Unstable chromatic homotopy theory
  • Stable motivic homotopy
  • Computer algorithms for cohomology of Hopf algebroids
  • Equivariant stable homotopy theory
  • Topological cyclic homology

Drafts

  • The algebraic Goodwillie tower
    • An attempt to understand the Goodwillie derivatives of the unstable Adams tower via the lambda algebra.
  • On the K(2)-local unstable homotopy groups of S^3 at p≥5
    • Computes unstable homotopy groups of the 3-sphere after apllying the K(2)-local Bousfield-Kuhn functor.
  • Homotopy of the K(2)-local Goodwillie derivatives of spheres
    • Investigates the Goodwillie derivatives of spheres: BP-homology, cellular structure, secondary and higher suspension, action of the Morava stabalizer group.
  • The monochromatic stable Hopf invariant
    • Gives a formula for the James-Hopf map, after applying the Bousfield-Kuhn functor, on Morava E-theory. Computes the monochromatic stable Hopf invariants of the beta families.

    Notes and Surveys

    • Topological cyclic homology of local fields
      • Slides on the descent spectral sequence for topological cyclic homology and computations of TC for local fields.
    • Prisms and Topological Cyclic Homology of Intergers
      • Slides on computing the toplogical cyclic homology of the integers using quasi-syntomic resolutions.
    • Motivic Ctau Modules
      • Slides for the equivalence of motivic Ctau modules and the derived category of BP_*BP comodules.
    • Dyer-Lashof Operations
      • Slides for the constructions and basic properties of the Dyer-Lashof operations.
    • Unstable Homotopy Theory from the Chromatic Point of View
      • Slides for chromatic unstable homotopy theory.
    • Survey of Computations of Homotopy Groups of Spheres and Cobordisms
      • Survey of methods used to compute the homotopy groups of spheres: framed cobordism, EHP sequence, Adams spectral sequence, complex cobordism and chromatic methods.
    • Periodic Homotopy Theory of Unstable Spheres
      • Introduction of what I intended to do in my thesis.
    • Notes for motivic decompositions
